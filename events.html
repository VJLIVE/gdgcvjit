Induction Program

225
Sept 28, 2024 09:30am - 12:30pm

The GDG VJIT Induction Event was held on September 28, 2024, marking the official commencement of the new tenure for the Google Developer Group Campus (GDG) at VJIT. The event aimed to welcome new members, introduce the core team, and provide a comprehensive overview of the club's vision and future initiatives. Hosted by GDG Design Lead Yashasvi and DSS Design Lead Himabala, the session featured a powerful address by Amena Yasmeen, the Organizer of GDG VJIT 2024–2025, who shared inspiring insights about the club’s mission to foster a vibrant and collaborative space for students passionate about technology, innovation, and learning. Dr. D. Aruna Kumari, HOD of CSE, also addressed the gathering with her encouraging words. The event included team introductions, an interactive Q&A session, and a detailed walkthrough of upcoming events, workshops, and opportunities for student involvement throughout the year.

Info Session

100
Dec 23, 2024 12:30pm - 03:30pm

The Solutions Challenge Info Session organized by the GDG team for 1st-year students served as an engaging introduction to the world of Web Development and App Development. Held in the VJIT lab and attended by 100 enthusiastic first-year students, the session provided a clear understanding of front-end and back-end technologies, essential tools, and modern development frameworks. The event also featured an overview of the Solution Challenge—Google’s flagship global competition—encouraging students to build impactful tech solutions aligned with the Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs). Attendees gained insights into current industry trends, career opportunities, and valuable learning resources to kickstart their development journey. The session concluded with a dynamic Q&A, where GDG mentors addressed queries and guided students on how to begin building real-world projects.

Python For AI

196
April 15, 2025 2 days

The Python for Beginners event was specially curated for first-year students who were new to programming. This session served as an introductory stepping stone into the world of programming using Python, one of the most popular and beginner-friendly languages in the tech industry. The event was designed to demystify basic programming concepts and to encourage a hands-on understanding of how Python could be used for real-world problem solving. It was conducted in an interactive format, encouraging questions and discussion to build a solid foundation for every student.
